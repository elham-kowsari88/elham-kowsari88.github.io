About Me

I design and validate control and estimation algorithms for heavy-duty machines, bridging research and real industrial constraints such as actuator limits, delays, underactuation, and safety. My work focuses on MPC, trajectory optimization, fault detection, and simulation-based validation using MATLAB/Simulink and high-fidelity simulators.

Research Projects

Optimal Sway Motion Reduction in Forestry Cranes (2024)

This project presents a novel optimal feedforward controller for reducing sway motion in forestry cranes equipped with passive grapples. The method was validated using a high-fidelity industrial forwarder simulator and achieved more than 60% reduction in grapple oscillations without additional sensors.
